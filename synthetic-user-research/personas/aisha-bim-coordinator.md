## Standards literacy

- **AIA G202 — Building Information Modeling Protocol Form.** LOD definitions cold: LOD 100 (concept), 200 (approximate geometry), 300 (precise dimensioned geometry), 350 (LOD 300 + interference detection across systems), 400 (fabrication-ready).
- **ISO 19650** — international BIM standard, increasingly required on federal/public projects.
- **Penn State BIM Project Execution Planning Guide** — used as starting template for BEPs.
- **COBie (Construction Operations Building Information Exchange)** — mandated on some projects for handover to facilities.
- **SMACNA** — knows that pressure class influences gauge, reinforcement spacing, and clearance envelope. Doesn't read SMACNA tables herself; relies on the mech sub.
- **NFPA 90A (HVAC)**, **13 (sprinkler)**, **70 (electrical)** — for clash-relevance and code coordination.

## Daily workflow

Assigned to a $200M hospital project, in coordination phase. Receives the design model from the MEP firm of record. Builds and maintains the federated Navisworks model with structural, architectural, MEP, FP. Runs weekly clash detection; tags clashes by trade pair and severity. Hosts coordination meetings with subs (mech, plumbing, FP, electrical) — typical attendance 8–12 people, weekly cadence. Tracks 200–500 open coordination issues at any one time. Reviews shop drawings against contract drawings as they come in. Coordinates installation sequencing with the field super on a 2-week look-ahead.

## Where the proposed product touches her work

- **Drawing/model drift.** When the engineer issues a 2D revision but the model isn't synced (or vice versa), the 2D drawing is the ground truth and she has to reconcile.
- **Shop drawing vs. contract drawing comparison.** Mech sub's shop drawings show pressure class and dimensions; she audits compliance against contract documents.
- **Clash-envelope decisions.** Heavy-gauge medium-pressure ducts have intermediate reinforcement that protrudes 1–2" beyond the duct face — affects clearance to ceiling grid, structural webs, fire separation assemblies.

## Initial pain framing (researcher hypothesis)

- Drawing/model drift across revisions. Manual reconciliation eats hours per cycle.
- Sub markup PDFs come back inconsistent — different conventions per sub.
- Hard to audit "is the sub's shop drawing actually compliant with the contract pressure class?" — requires cross-reading the spec.
- RFI cycle is 5–10 days; she has to make coordination decisions sooner than that.
