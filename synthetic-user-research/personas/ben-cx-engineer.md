# Persona Card — Ben Okonkwo, CxA

**Role:** Independent commissioning agent / sole proprietor
**Firm type and size:** Self-employed, three subcontractor staff for site work. Solo book of business.
**Tenure / certifications:** 10 years in Cx, 5 years prior as a TAB technician. ASHRAE-certified Commissioning Process Management Professional (CPMP). LEED AP O+M.
**Geography:** Pacific Northwest. Mixed portfolio: ~50% new construction Cx, ~50% retrocommissioning of existing buildings.

## Standards literacy

- **ASHRAE Standard 111 — Practices for Measurement, Testing, Adjusting, and Balancing of Building HVAC Systems.** Governs TAB; he reviews TAB reports against this.
- **ASHRAE Standard 215 — Method of Test for Determining Duct Leakage.** Increasingly cited on new specs; replaces older SMACNA-only protocols on many projects.
- **SMACNA Duct Air Leakage Test Manual.** Knows leakage classes (3, 6, 12, 24, 48 CFM/100 sq ft at 1" w.g. test pressure). Allowable leakage rate scales with construction pressure class — that's why pressure class matters for his pass/fail.
- **ASHRAE Guideline 0 — The Commissioning Process.** Foundational document for how he organizes engagements.
- **ASHRAE Guideline 1.1 — HVAC&R Technical Requirements for the Commissioning Process.**
- **IMC, IECC, ASHRAE 90.1** — for retrocommissioning baseline assessment.

## Daily tools

- **CxAlloy** — issue tracking and Cx documentation. SaaS, ~$120/user/month. He pays for it himself.
- **Bluebeam Revu** — drawing markup, especially for retrocommissioning where drawings are scanned.
- **Excel** — duct inventory templates (his own), TAB report cross-checks, equipment schedules.
- **Procore** — when the GC requires it (occasional).
- **Field instruments:** pitot tube traverse, balometer (capture hood), tape measure, hygrometer, thermal anemometer.
- **Matterport / BLK360** — 3D scanning for retrocommissioning when drawings are unavailable. Rents the BLK360 occasionally.

## Daily workflow

- **New construction Cx.** Writes the Cx plan during DD. Performs design review (flags constructability issues). Witnesses TAB testing. Witnesses functional performance testing. Reviews trend logs. Writes the Cx report. Engagement length: 18–24 months from DD to substantial completion.
- **Retrocommissioning.** Site survey + drawing review (drawings often poor scans of pre-2000 originals or missing entirely). Monitoring period (weeks to months of trend data). Recommends repairs and operational improvements. Issues retrocommissioning report. Engagement length: 3–9 months.
- Retro is harder than new construction — drawings are often missing or unusable, so he field-rebuilds the duct inventory before he can plan testing scope.

## Where the proposed product touches his work

- **New construction.** Contract drawings drive the Cx checklist. Pressure class is an input to leakage-test pass/fail criteria.
- **Retrocommissioning.** Re-creating an as-built duct inventory from old PDFs is brutal. The drawings are often the only artifact. This is the workflow with the *lowest* tolerance for manual extraction because the alternative is a tape-measure site survey.

## Initial pain framing (researcher hypothesis)

- Old drawings are poor scans. OCR quality is the binding constraint on automation.
- Pressure class often missing from pre-2000 drawings (wasn't always called out per duct).
- Field-vs-drawing discrepancies are common; needs both sides structured to compare.
- Tools are fragmented — Excel, Bluebeam, CxAlloy. No single inventory of truth.
- Retrocommissioning engagements are bid against a fixed fee. Inventory-building eats the margin.

## Buying authority

- Discretionary band: ~$200/mo per user. He's the buyer.
- Approval threshold: ~$5K/yr — he'd think hard but it's still his decision.
- No final decision-maker above him — sole proprietor.
