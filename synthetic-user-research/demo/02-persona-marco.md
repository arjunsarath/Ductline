# Persona Card — Marco Reyes

**Role:** Estimating principal / fab shop manager at a mechanical sub-contractor
**Firm type and size:** Privately-held mechanical sub, ~50 employees, southeastern US. SMACNA contractor member.
**Tenure / certifications:** 18 years estimating, last 6 also running fab shop. NCCER-certified. Started as a sheet-metal apprentice.
**Geography:** Southeastern US. Bids commercial: schools, mid-rise office, light industrial, occasional healthcare.

## Standards literacy

- **SMACNA HVAC Duct Construction Standards (4th ed., 2020).** Reads Tables 2-1 through 2-50 (rectangular gauge / reinforcement) and Tables 3-1 through 3-7 (round gauge) cold. Knows that a 36×24 rectangular at 2" w.g. construction calls 18 ga; same duct at 1" calls 20 ga.
- **SMACNA Duct Air Leakage Test Manual.** Aware of leakage classes 3, 6, 12, 24, 48 — though leakage testing is usually run by the TAB sub, not him.
- **ASHRAE 90.1 duct leakage compliance** — touches his work because gauge selection on the bid has to match the spec'd leakage class.
- **IMC 603** for duct construction code reference.
- Uses pressure class shorthand: Low (≤2" w.g.), Medium (>2" to 6"), High (>6"), but recognizes the underlying SMACNA spec is in specific increments (½", 1", 2", 3", 4", 6", 10").

## Daily tools

- **PlanSwift** — primary on-screen takeoff tool; ten years of muscle memory.
- **Trimble Estimation MEP** — used selectively when the customer specs it.
- **TaksoAI** — adopted ~12 months ago for duct quantity. Uses for the geometry; reconciles pressure class himself.
- **Bluebeam Revu** — review and markup, secondary measurement.
- **Excel** — bid summary, pricing buildup.
- **COMPASS / FabriCAD** for fab shop CNC programming.
- **Procore** — submittals and RFIs on awarded jobs.

## Daily workflow

Wakes up to a stack of bid invitations. Triages by job size, GC relationship, current backlog. Major bid: 50–200 sheets, 60–80 hours of takeoff over 2–3 weeks. Splits work with one or two junior estimators; reviews their takeoff before pricing. Pricing is per-pound for sheet metal driven by gauge (which is driven by pressure class). Submits bid; if won, kicks off submittal package and shop drawing process. Fab shop programs cut lists from his takeoff. Marco walks the floor twice a day, catches gauge errors before they go to coil.

## Where the proposed product touches his work

The takeoff stage. Specifically, the "I have a duct identified and need to assign size and pressure class to it" step. Today: TaksoAI gives him size + run identification. He reads the title-block schedule per system tag, then propagates pressure class to each segment manually. Dimension callouts on the drawing get cross-checked against the TaksoAI output.

## Initial pain framing (researcher hypothesis)

- Pressure class is the main remaining friction after TaksoAI handled quantity.
- Ambiguous pressure class triggers conservative gauge padding that erodes win-rate.
- Mid-bid clarifications (RFIs) come back too slowly to act on.
- Junior estimator pressure-class errors surface in fab.

## Buying authority

- Discretionary band: ~$200/mo per user without approval.
- Approval threshold: ~$2,000/mo or any annual contract over $20K.
- Final decision-maker if above threshold: him + the firm's owner / president (often the same conversation).
